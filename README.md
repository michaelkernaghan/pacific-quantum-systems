# Pacific Quantum Systems

Research initiative based in Vancouver, BC, investigating the mathematical foundations of quantum mechanics and the questions they raise about physical reality.

**Website:** [pacific-quantum-systems.com](https://pacific-quantum-systems.com/)

## Research Areas

### Quantum Contextuality (Foundations)

Algebraic classification of Kochen-Specker sets in dimension three. Our central result is that generator norm ≤ 2 controls KS-uncolorability — the integer identity 1+1=2, the Peres identity (√2)²=2, and the Eisenstein identity 1+ω+ω²=0 are all manifestations of the same algebraic constraint. Six constructions from algebraic number rings have been identified, including two genuinely new KS sets (Heegner-7 and Golden ratio) not in any prior catalogue.

### Post-Quantum Cryptography

Tracking the transition to quantum-resistant algorithms. Coverage of NIST standards (ML-DSA/FIPS 204, ML-KEM/FIPS 203, SLH-DSA/FIPS 205), lattice-based cryptography, and blockchain integration — particularly Tezos tz5 addresses based on ML-DSA (Dilithium).

### Quantum Communications

Quantum key distribution protocols (BB84, E91, CV-QKD), the Canadian quantum communications landscape (NRC QUIN, Photonic Inc., TELUS, SFU Q-Van Lab, QEYSSat), and the classical-quantum software interface enabling secure network infrastructure.

## Computation

Computational methods — SAT solvers, graph algorithms, algebraic search, and integer linear programming — serve as instruments for foundational discovery. Key tools: Python, PySAT (Glucose4), NumPy/SciPy, CVXPY, NetworkX.
